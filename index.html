IS MONEY

Our Nerds' thoughts on June's most important money news and goals.

We were told there would be frivolous spending

Remember when money kept magically appearing and there was nothing to spend it on so we saved it in stocks that kept going up? Yeah, that’s over.

The cost of everything — from travel to tacos — continues to surge. That’s driving down household spending power, which is a jargony way of saying, “it’s hard to buy stuff.”

Financing your fun

Not cool, macroeconomic trends. Not cool. This was supposed to be the summer where we flew first class, not the one where we held a garage sale to cover the cost of A/C repair.

What’s a financially responsible-ish person to do?

Tightening our financial belts is one option. Turning to vacation loans, buy-now-pay-later plans or financing travel with a credit card are others. But while these all have their place, it generally stinks to pay for a vacation after you get home.

Budgets don’t have to mean buzzkill

The choice between “splurge on a dream vacation” and “stay home and eat beans” is a false one. There are plenty of ways to budget for a dream summer that don’t involve lighting money on fire.

Yes, travel this summer is going to be expensive on average, but there are still deals to be found.
